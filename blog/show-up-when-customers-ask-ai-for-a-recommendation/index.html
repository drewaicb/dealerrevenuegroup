AI Visibility · GEO

How to Show Up When Customers Ask AI for a Recommendation

More and more buyers no longer open ten browser tabs. They ask ChatGPT, Google AI, Claude, or Perplexity a plain question — "who's the best place near me to do X?" — and act on the short list it gives back. If your business isn't in that answer, you never even enter the consideration set. Generative engine optimization (GEO) is the work of getting named in those answers.

Why this is different from Google rankings

Classic SEO is about ranking a page in a list of blue links the customer then scans. GEO is about being named in a synthesized answer the customer largely trusts as-is. The AI doesn't show ten options — it names a few, in a sentence or two, and the buyer moves on. Position one in a list of ten is good; being one of the three names the model says out loud is the new game.

Where AI models actually get their answers

Generative models don't invent local recommendations from nothing. They assemble them from the public signals they can find about you and your competitors:

  • Your own web presence — clear, current pages that plainly state who you are, where you are, and what you do.
  • Third-party profiles & directories — your business listing, maps profile, and category directories, with consistent details.
  • Reviews and ratings — both the score and the language people use about you.
  • Forums and communities — Reddit and similar threads where people ask for and give recommendations.
  • News, articles, and citations — being referenced by other credible sources.

The key shift: you can't edit the AI's answer directly. You influence it by improving the public signals it reads — and the first step is knowing what it currently says.

A practical GEO checklist

  1. Find out what AI says about you today. Ask the major models the questions your buyers ask — "best [your category] near [your city]?" Note whether you're named, how you're described, and who shows up instead.
  2. Make your core facts unmistakable. Name, location, categories, hours, and what you offer should appear in plain language on your own site and your main profiles — and match everywhere.
  3. Answer real buyer questions in plain text. Models favor content that directly answers the question a customer would ask. Write the way people search out loud.
  4. Earn and shape reviews. Volume, recency, and the words customers use all feed the model's description of you. Encourage detailed reviews; respond to them.
  5. Show up in the communities buyers trust. When people ask for recommendations in forums, being mentioned honestly and helpfully matters.
  6. Track it over time. AI answers change weekly. GEO isn't a one-time fix — it's a signal you watch and improve, the way you'd watch a key sales metric.

You can't improve what you can't see

The hardest part of GEO is that the answer is invisible unless you go looking. A competitor can quietly become the default recommendation in your market and you'd never know from your own dashboard.
