GEO vs SEO: What Local Businesses Need to Know About AI Search

For twenty years, "getting found" meant ranking on Google. Now a growing share of your customers never see a list of links at all — they ask ChatGPT, Google's AI Overviews, Claude, or Perplexity a question and act on the answer. If those models don't mention your business, you're invisible at the exact moment someone is deciding. That's the gap GEO is built to close.

What is GEO?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization — the practice of influencing how AI engines describe, recommend, and rank your business when someone asks them a question. Where SEO is about ranking a page in a list of blue links, GEO is about being the answer the AI gives.

When a buyer types "best BMW service near Madison" or "which med spa in town is most reputable" into ChatGPT, the model returns a short, confident recommendation. GEO is the work of making sure your business is in that recommendation — accurately, and ahead of your competitor.

GEO vs SEO: the core differences

  • The result format. SEO competes for a ranked list of links. GEO competes for a single synthesized answer — often with only two or three businesses named.
  • The source material. Google rewards your own optimized pages. AI engines pull from across the web — your site, yes, but also reviews, Reddit threads, directories, news, and forums. Your reputation is the raw material.
  • The click. SEO success is a click to your site. GEO success is often a mention with no click — the customer trusts the AI's summary and contacts you directly.
  • The feedback loop. With SEO you can check your rank. With GEO, the answer changes by model, by phrasing, and over time — so you have to actively monitor what each engine is saying.
  • Winner-take-most. Page two of Google still gets traffic. Being the fourth business an AI "would have mentioned" gets you nothing.

The short version: SEO gets you into the list. GEO gets you into the answer. Local businesses still need both — but almost no one is managing the second one yet, which is exactly why it's an opportunity.

Why local businesses can't ignore this

AI assistants are now the first stop for a large and growing share of "who should I use / where should I go" research. Three things make this urgent for local operators specifically:

  1. The answers are confident even when they're wrong. An AI may name an old competitor, cite a stale review, or skip you entirely — and the customer has no reason to doubt it.
  2. You don't see it happening. There's no analytics dashboard telling you "the AI recommended your competitor 40 times this week." The loss is silent.
  3. Your competitors don't see it either. That means the operator who measures and manages their AI narrative first gets a real head start.

How to improve your GEO (the organic basics)

You can't pay an AI model for placement, but you can shape the inputs it reads. The fundamentals:

  • Earn and respond to reviews. AI engines lean heavily on review volume, recency, and sentiment. Consistent, recent, well-handled reviews are GEO fuel.
  • Keep your facts consistent everywhere. Name, location, services, and hours should match across your site, Google Business Profile, and directories. Contradictions make models hedge or omit you.
  • Publish clear, question-shaped content. Pages that directly answer the questions customers ask (like this one) are easier for models to quote.
  • Watch the social layer. Reddit and forum threads carry outsized weight in AI answers. A single unanswered complaint can define your narrative.
  • Monitor continuously. Because answers drift, GEO is a maintenance practice, not a one-time fix.
